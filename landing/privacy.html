Privacy Policy

Updated: April 17, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Eigen Labs, Inc., operating the Darkbloom platform, collects, uses, discloses, and otherwise processes personal information in connection with Darkbloom's websites, console, APIs, software, provider applications, hosted services, and related products and features (collectively, the "Services").

Standalone Document. This Privacy Policy is a standalone document governing the Darkbloom Services only. It is separate from and does not incorporate the Eigen Labs Privacy Policy or other privacy notices governing EigenLayer, EigenCloud, EigenDA, EigenCompute, or other Eigen Labs products.

1. Scope

This policy applies when you:

  • Visit our websites or console
  • Create or use accounts, API keys, wallet-linked sessions, or device-link flows
  • Submit prompts, files, images, or audio to the Services
  • Install or operate provider software or the macOS app
  • Communicate with us about the Services

2. Personal Information We Collect

2.1 Account and identity information: Name, business details, email address, authentication identifiers from identity providers including Privy user identifiers, wallet addresses and related wallet identifiers, API keys, and tax identification information.

2.2 Payment, transaction, and billing information: Billing session identifiers, payment method type, deposit and withdrawal information, transaction identifiers, blockchain transaction signatures, wallet addresses, and related public-ledger information.

2.3 Usage and service metadata: Request identifiers, account and provider identifiers, selected model or feature, token counts, audio duration, image count, timestamps, per-request cost or billing metadata, API route, status code, and operational metrics.

2.4 Content you submit and receive: Prompts, messages, instructions, files, attachments, audio for transcription, image-generation prompts, generated images, model outputs and responses.

2.5 Provider and device information: Hardware and device characteristics (machine model, chip family, memory, CPU/GPU information, available capacity), provider wallet addresses, attestation materials and security data (Secure Enclave-generated P-256 public keys, ECDSA attestation signatures, SHA-256 hashes of provider binary, hardware serial numbers, SIP status, Secure Boot level, Authenticated Root Volume integrity status, challenge-response verification data), heartbeats, health checks, thermal state, memory pressure, security posture, version information, device-linking tokens, and account-link status. MDM enrollment is read-only security verification only.

2.6 Technical and log information: IP address and network information, request logs, device, browser, and operating system information, local storage or client-side state, cookies, SDK storage, or similar session technologies.

2.7 Communications and support information: Contents of messages, attachments, and related contact details.

3. How We Collect Personal Information

We collect personal information directly from you, automatically when you use the Services, from service providers and partners, and from public sources including public blockchain records.

4. How We Use Personal Information

We use personal information to provide, operate, maintain, secure, and improve the Services; authenticate users and devices; route requests and support billing; operate provider onboarding and related features; collect tax information and fulfill tax reporting obligations; communicate with users; monitor performance and reliability; investigate and enforce compliance; comply with law; and create aggregated or de-identified information.

5. Important Content-Handling Disclosures

5.1 Transit and relay: Content is transmitted over TLS and may be encrypted before relaying to providers.

5.2 Coordinator access: The coordinator processes request payloads in plaintext on a transient basis.

5.3 Logging: Coordinator code is not designed to log prompt content in ordinary request logs, but operational metadata is logged.

5.4 Selected providers: Content is disclosed to the selected provider to fulfill inference requests.

5.5 Images and uploads: Generated images may be temporarily stored in service memory.

5.6 Training: This Privacy Policy does not grant us the right to use your Content for model training. If we decide to do so in the future, we will update this Privacy Policy.

6. How We Disclose Personal Information

We may disclose personal information to service providers and subprocessors, identity/wallet/payment partners, providers participating in the network, your organization if you are using the Services through a company account, law enforcement, regulators, or courts when required by law, in connection with mergers or acquisitions, and with your direction or consent. We do not sell personal information for money or share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

7. Legal Bases

We process personal information on the bases of performance of contract, legitimate interests, compliance with legal obligations, and your consent where required.

8. Retention

We retain personal information as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy, including maintaining records, investigating incidents, enforcing agreements, and satisfying legal requirements. Retention periods vary by data type.

9. Your Privacy Choices and Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to know and access personal information we hold about you, correct inaccurate information, request deletion, receive a portable copy, opt out of certain processing, limit use or disclosure of sensitive personal information, and receive non-discriminatory treatment. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request. Contact notices@eigenlabs.org for privacy requests.

10. Cookies, Local Storage, and Similar Technologies

We use these technologies to keep you signed in, store API keys and configuration choices, remember preferences, support wallet and authentication SDK functionality, and secure sessions. You can control these through your browser settings, though disabling them may affect functionality.

11. Third-Party Sites, Wallets, and Social Features

The Services may link to or interoperate with third-party sites and services. Information you share with those third parties is governed by their own terms and privacy practices, not ours.

12. Security

We use administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect personal information. No security measures can guarantee absolute protection, and we cannot guarantee that information will never be accessed, disclosed, altered, or destroyed.

13. International Transfers

We may process personal information in the United States and other jurisdictions. When transferring personal information internationally, we rely on appropriate legal mechanisms as required by applicable law.

14. Children's Privacy

The Services are not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us at notices@eigenlabs.org.

15. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy. Material changes will be posted with an updated effective date. Continued use of the Services after changes become effective constitutes acceptance.
